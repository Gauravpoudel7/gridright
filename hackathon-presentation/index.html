The Problem

Your solar panels sit idle half the day.

A typical rooftop produces more energy than a home can use — especially at noon, when the sun is strongest.

That extra energy? It just goes to waste.

A household that produces and consumes its own energy is called a prosumerTERM. Today, prosumers have no good way to share their surplus.

Meanwhile, your neighbor is buying power from the grid at full price.

Sun is free. Power is not.
The gap between them is lost money for everyone.

Net meteringTERM — the mechanism most countries use to credit surplus solar — pays a fraction of the retail electricity price. Communities deserve better.

2 / 12
The Hidden Problem

No easy way to share solar between neighbors.

  • Selling energy back to the grid via feed-in tariffTERM pays you pennies — not real value.
  • Sending surplus to a specific neighbor? Almost impossible with today's grid infrastructure.
  • Communities want to pool their DERsDistributed Energy Resources — rooftops, batteries, EVs — but the tools don't exist yet.
  • Energy bills rise every year, even when the sun is shining on your block.

50% of people can't put panels on their roof — renters, apartments, shaded homes. They are locked out of the solar revolution entirely.

AI Architecture — The Human-in-the-Loop Principle

AI recommends. Operator decides.

🤖 What the AI engine does

  • Ingests real-time pool state — current supply, demand, and price signals
  • Runs time-series forecasts on solar production curves
  • Ranks pool dispatch options: who should export, who should import, and at what rate
  • Flags anomalies to the exception queue for human review

👤 What the operator does

  • Reviews the AI's ranked recommendations — never receives a done deal
  • Manages the exception queue: disputes, anomalies, out-of-band trades
  • Approves or rejects each dispatch — the final call is always a human
  • Sets community pool policy: price floor, max draw per household

Why this matters: Autonomous AI settlement is how you end up on the front page. Trust is the moat — and trust requires a human in the loop.

What We Built

Three things, working together.

A

Pool App — Next.js + Supabase

Web dashboard where neighbors sign up, see their kWh contribution, and track savings. Secured with Supabase Row-Level Security (RLS) — each user sees only their own data.

B

Smart Engine — Python FastAPI + AI

AI recommends the best pool dispatch between sellers and buyers. A human operator reviews every recommendation via the exception queue — HITL by design. The AI never makes the final call.

C

Settlement Layer — Anchor + Bubblegum

Every P2P trade is recorded on the Solana blockchain via a custom Anchor program. Prosumers earn cNFT badges (Metaplex Bubblegum protocol) at contribution milestones — 100 kWh earns your first badge.
